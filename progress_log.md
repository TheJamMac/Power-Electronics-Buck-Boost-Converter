# Project Progress Log

This document records the development progress of the DC-DC converter project.

## Initial Project Setup

The project was started with the objective of studying the behavior of DC-DC power converters, specifically Buck and Boost converters.

The first step was defining the scope of the project and identifying the main goals of the work.

Key tasks completed in this stage:

* created the GitHub repository
* defined the overall project structure
* wrote the project description and documentation
* prepared folders for simulations, results, PCB work, and documentation

## Converter Study and Research

Before starting the circuit design, basic theory related to Buck and Boost converters was reviewed.

Topics studied include:

* switching converter fundamentals
* duty cycle and voltage relationship
* inductor energy storage behavior
* capacitor filtering and ripple reduction

This stage helped establish the theoretical foundation for the simulation work.

## LTspice Simulation Development

After the theoretical preparation, the Buck converter circuit was implemented and simulated in LTspice.

During the simulation phase:

* the converter schematic was designed
* PWM switching behavior was analyzed
* inductor current waveform was observed
* output voltage ripple was examined

Simulation results were documented and waveform screenshots were added to the repository.

## Current Status

At this stage, the Buck converter simulation has been successfully implemented and documented.

The next development step will be the **PCB layout design of the converter circuit** and further improvements to the project documentation.
